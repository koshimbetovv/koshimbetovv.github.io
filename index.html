About

Mathematics, graphs, and real-world network problems.

My background is in mathematics, with an MSc thesis on the minimum number of filled cells in maximal partial Latin hypercubes. During my graduate studies, my interests expanded toward graph learning and network-based methods for financial crime detection.

I enjoy working at the intersection of rigorous mathematical thinking and computational experimentation. I am especially interested in how graph structure can reveal meaningful patterns in large transaction and blockchain networks.

Research

Current research directions

01

AML transaction networks

I study methods for extracting compact, illicit-rich subgraphs from transaction networks, with a focus on feed-guided shortest paths, motif-based feed selection, and interpretable graph mining strategies.

02

Graph neural networks

I work with GNN models for fraud and illicit activity detection, including static and temporal graph learning settings on financial and blockchain networks.

03

Adversarial robustness

I am interested in robustness of graph learning systems under adversarial perturbations, especially insertion-based attacks that respect transaction-network constraints.

Doniyor Koshimbetov, Emine Şule Yazıcı, and Asha Rao
